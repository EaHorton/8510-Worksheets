I spent some time looking at each question – extraneous advertisements & how promotion and reception changed over time – and I came away with a number of observations.

Feminine identifiers, such as “woman,” “girl,” and female names, appear in extraneous topic models and reception topic models, but never in promotion. “Boy” and “boys,” however, appear consistently in the promotion topic models. This implies that the Buffalo Bill show was not advertising itself to a female audience. But, it seems that women attended and engaged with the show anyway.

I am not sure if I am reading too far into this observation, but the inclusion of topic 11 in the post-01-01-1899 promotion topic model is interesting because of the word “evangelical.” The Second Great Awakening occurred in the early 19th century in the United States. It makes sense, therefore, that evangelism would seep its way into the Buffalo Bill show (if that is indeed what was happening).

Topic 13 of all promotion materials also caught my eye. It consists of “col,” “colonel,” “american,” “scenes,” “history.” As one of the most intuitive topics, I am led to believe that Cody’s company advertised their show as a history lesson of sorts.
